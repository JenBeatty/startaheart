WELCOME TO START A HEART BAKERSFIELD

Our Visionary

Stephen Cowan, a loved member of our community, was a passionate advocate of CPR. Known to all as “Mr. CPR,” Stephen spent countless volunteer hours training people in CPR. His wish was that they would gain valuable knowledge and share in his passion for this powerful tool that could help save lives. Having been an Emergency Medical Technician (E.M.T) for Hall Ambulance in his early career, an employee of Bakersfield Memorial Hospital, and an International Board Member of the American Heart Association – Stephen understood the needs of the community. It was more than his job, it was his mission in life to get individuals enthusiastic about CPR. His stories and dreams captivated audiences wherever he went; colleagues, friends and complete strangers were invigorated to action because of his giving heart.

Because of his passion for CPR and desire to teach others, he envisioned Start A Heart Bakersfield – an effort that would train individuals in CPR and place more AEDs in the community. His ultimate goal: to save more heartbeats. In the midst of his planning and preparation, the unexpected happened. In February of 2010, we lost Stephen – a dear friend and the man whose passion for CPR surpassed many. At his request, community friends came together to implement and build upon the Start A Heart Bakersfield name. The Start A Heart Bakersfield organization beats on because of Mr. CPR’s legacy.

Our Mission

To raise awareness and educate Kern County residents about sudden cardiac arrest (SCA), while expanding access to automated external defibrillators (AEDs) and cardiac pulmonary resuscitation (CPR) training to help save lives.

Our Goal

More than 325,000 lives are claimed each year from SCA. Start A Heart Bakersfield’s goal is to save heartbeats by populating the city with strategically-placed AEDs in accessible areas where they are most needed. Additionally, we hope to train individuals interested in learning CPR.
